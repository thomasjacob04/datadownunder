Land values in the Snowy Monaro Regional local government area

South East Regional NSW region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Snowy Monaro Regional area were prepared by Opteon Property Group Pty Ltd.

General overview

Overall, the Snowy Monaro local government area experienced a strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024. This increase was due to the benefits from being a central location to the Snowy Mountains, the ACT and South Coast, offering generally affordable housing for owner occupiers and investors. Additionally, this local government area benefits from a generally robust local jobs market with ongoing infrastructure projects serving as a major economic driver in the region.
This increase was not consistent across all sectors with the residential and commercial sectors remaining steady, the industrial experiencing a slight increase and the rural sector experiencing a strong increase.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

The residential sector for the Snowy Monaro local government area remained steady for the 2024 base year. this steady market was due to adequate supply of residential properties meeting to meet the strong demand for these properties from both local and out of town buyers. This increase was not consistent across the sector.
Low density residential zoned properties within Cooma experienced a moderate increase, as well as residential properties in Bombala and Adaminaby. Large lot residential zoned lifestyle properties within the Burra, Jindabyne and Moonbah localities also experienced a moderate increase. This increase can be attributed to the ongoing local economic growth of this region which was driven by the construction and tourism industries, as well as an increased supply to meet the high demand from both local and out of town buyers.
The villages of Nimmitabel, Delegate, Craigie and Bibbenluke experienced a strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024. Large lot residential zoned lifestyle properties within the Numeralla, Nimmitabel, Dalgety, Adaminaby, and Braemar Bay also experienced a strong increase. This increase was due to the appeal to live in a rural style village, however, still be within a close proximity to the Snowy Mountains, the ACT and the South Coast. The villages of Old Adaminaby and Anglers Reach experienced a very strong increase as well as large lot residential zoned lifestyle properties around Bombala, Berridale, Bredbo and Michelago.
General residential properties within Cooma experienced a moderate decrease, while general residential and low density properties in Central Jindabyne experienced a strong decrease. This decrease was due to an increase in supply with reduced demand, particularly for vacant land, and as a result of market correction after two years of very strong value increases.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, the rural sector in the Snowy Monaro local government area experienced a strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024. Market drivers for this increase can be attributed to the increase of livestock prices and ideal seasonal conditions. This increase was not consistent across the sector.
Grazing properties in the areas of southeast Cooma, Tuross, Nimmitabel, Creewah, Bibbenluke and Cathcart experienced a slight increase, as well as primary production industrial properties at Four Mile. This increase was due to buyers wishing to relocate from larger metropolitan areas to live a more rural lifestyle however still be within a close proximity to major centres such as Snowy Mountains, the ACT and the South Coast. As well as the increase of livestock prices and ideal seasonal conditions.
Environmental living zoned rural residential properties within the Cooma locality experienced a strong increase. Bush blocks in southeast Cooma, Tuross, Nimmitabel, Creewah, Bibbenluke and Cathcart localities, grazing properties in the areas of East Jindabyne, Kalkite, Adaminaby, Shannons Flat, Dry Plains and north Berridale experienced a very strong increase as well as grazing properties in the areas of Bombala, Gunningrach, Delegate, Corrowong, Mila and Craigie, rural grazing greater than 1000 Ha also experienced. This increase was due to more people working remotely, but still being within a close proximity to major centres, the desire for rural living continues to grow.
Hobby farms and rural bush blocks in the Jindabyne, Crackenback, Moonbah, Ingebirah, Jimenbuen and Bungarby localities remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024. Hobby farms, grazing properties and bush blocks in the southwest Cooma, Rock Flat, Springfield, Maffra, Dalgety, Avonside and south Berridale localities also remained steady as well as properties with no dwelling entitlements in the southeast Cooma, Tuross, Nimmitabel, Creewah, Bibbenluke, Cathcart, East Jindabyne, Kalkite, Adaminaby, Shannons Flat, Dry Plains and north Berridale localities. This steady market was due to adequate supply to meet demand from both local and out of town buyers. Windfarms, solar and utilities also remained steady.
Hobby farms over 40 Ha in the southeast Cooma, Tuross, Nimmitabel, Creewah, Bibbenluke and Cathcart localities experienced a slight decrease while hobby farms under 40 Ha in these localities experienced a moderate decrease. This was due to land owners holding on to their properties for a longer period, causing limited supply of these types of properties. Radiata pine plantations also experienced a moderate decrease.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
