General overview

Overall, the Newcastle local government area showed a slight increase in land values between July 2023 and July 2024.

However, this trend was not consistent across every property sector. Residential land values experienced an overall slight increase while commercial and industrial land values remained steady.

Population growth and a strong demand for residential housing in the state of NSW has underpinned demand for residential property in Newcastle supported by its commuting distance from Sydney.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential properties in the Newcastle local government area showed an overall slight increase in land values between July 2023 and July 2024 however this was not consistent across all areas.

Low density residential zoned properties in parts of Wallsend, Elermore Vale and Warabrook experienced strong increases due to high demand for relatively affordable properties in close proximity to the Newcastle CBD. Properties in Beresfield and Tarro also experienced strong increases due to their proximity to major infrastructure projects and their relative affordability within the Newcastle local government area.

Low density and environmental conservation zoned properties in Maryland experienced strong increases. This was due to strong demand and limited supply of small acreage lifestyle properties in close proximity to the Newcastle CBD.

A pocket of low density residential zoned properties in Lambton and New Lambton, east of Croudace Street, experienced a strong increase. These properties are near Lambton Park, Lambton swimming pool and the popular Elder Street retail strip and generally have good character dwellings in close proximity to the Newcastle CBD. In contrast, parts of Lambton, New Lambton, New Lambton Heights, North Lambton, Jesmond and Waratah and medium density properties in New Lambton close to the railway line experienced slight decreases.

Low density residential properties in parts of Hamilton North, Adamstown and Mayfield (south of Maitland Road) as well as medium density zoned properties in Adamstown, Adamstown Heights and Broadmeadow experienced strong increases. These areas have excellent surrounding educational, recreational and retail amenities and are within very close proximity to the Newcastle CBD.

Low density and medium density zoned residential exception type properties located across the local government area including some schools, churches, heritage listed properties and constrained land used for public purposes or as curtilage experienced strong increases.

Medium density residential zoned properties in Newcastle East strongly increased. This is located in the Newcastle CBD where vacant land is rare and supply is limited. Greater development opportunities here usually rely on the demolition of existing structures. In contrast, medium density properties in the broader areas of Cooks Hill, The Junction and The Hill experienced slight decreases where demand appeared to have slightly softened.

Mixed use development zoned properties in the Newcastle city centre experienced a moderate decrease along with mixed use in Wickham which slightly decreased. Decreases in these areas are due to high development costs and reduced market activity.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Overall, commercial land values were steady in the Newcastle local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This was not consistent in all commercial market segments.

Commercial sites with a Local Centre zoning in locations including Adamstown, Carrington, Elermore Vale, Hamilton South, Kotara, Mayfield, New Lambton, Wallsend and Warabrook showed a moderate decrease. Commercial sites with a Local Centre zoning in locations including Hamilton and Islington showed a strong decrease. Commercial sites with a Productivity Support zoning in locations including Broadmeadow, Lambton, New Lambton and Waratah showed a moderate decrease. These areas experienced less demand than supply from both owner occupiers and tenants.

Commercial sites with a Productivity Support zoning in locations including Adamstown, Kotara and Merewether showed a moderate increase. These areas experienced more demand than supply from both owner occupiers and tenants.

Newcastle is the regional capital of the Hunter Region and benefits from being one of the major NSW Regional economies outside of Sydney. The district is supported by a broad industry base, including a major Australian port and a substantial central business district.

Newcastle has seen a population increase with regional migration and some overseas migration.

Commercial land values, underpinned by demand from a broad-based diversified local economy and continued population growth, were broadly steady despite the effects of recent significant increase in interest rates.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
