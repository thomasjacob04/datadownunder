General overview

There was an overall moderate increase in land values for the Georges River local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This trend was generally consistent across the residential market segment, with the exception of industrial land values which showed strong increases largely driven by the lack of supply and strong demand for properties within the industrial sector, whilst the commercial sector showed a slight increase in land values due to the more subdued market sentiment shown for commercial zoned properties.

The overall moderate increase in land values was largely driven by a stabilisation in interest rates and a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year, the strong increase in industrial land values was largely driven by greater demand versus limited supply coming to the market.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

There was a slight increase overall across commercial land values in the Georges River local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This trend can be attributed to the driven by a stabilisation in interest rates and a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year, the strong increase in land values was largely driven by greater demand versus limited supply coming to the market.

This trend however was not consistent, with E2 zoned narrow site commercial properties in Hurstville showing strong increases in land value which can be attributed to the high demand for more entry level smaller properties within the Hurstville CBD. Mixed use commercial / residential properties in Hurstville and Kogarah also showed steady changes in land value which can be attributed to the more subdued high density residential unit market and coupled with increasing stock levels as new projects come to the market.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

There was a strong increase overall in industrial land values between July 2023 and July 2024. This can be attributed to the reduced supply of industrial land due to properties being rezoned for residential development across Metropolitan Sydney together with infrastructure projects.

The reduced supply of suitable zoned industrial land, especially within the established Kingsgrove industrial area which saw very limited sales of existing stock, and tight market conditions has had a positive impact on land values for the remaining industrial land within the local government area.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
