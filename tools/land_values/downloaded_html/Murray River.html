General overview

Land values in the Murray River local government area showed a slight increase between July 2023 and July 2024. The increase in land values was not consistent across the market segments. Residential land values showed a strong decrease, commercial values remained steady, industrial and rural values showed a moderate increase.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall have shown a slight decrease. The residential areas of Moama continued to expand with additional residential estates being developed. However, demand slowed causing a shift in land values in a downward direction. Low density residential, and large lot residential areas in Moama showed slight increases. The general residential areas and the large lot residential areas within the township of Barham also showed moderate increases whilst the River Frontage residential properties in Barham showed a slight increase. The village of Murray Downs had significant demand again for river access properties pushing the land values to a very strong increase.
The village areas and Large Lot Residential areas overall showed a slight increase. The village of Wakool showed a very strong increase whilst the village of Mathoura showed a moderate increase. Purchasers are attracted to the larger lot sizes available, quiet location, and relatively affordable housing in these villages.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial values overall have shown moderate increases. Purchasers of industrial properties are generally owner occupiers who operate businesses within the community. The industrial areas in Moama generally showed moderate increases after similar increases in 2023. Purchasers are attracted to Barham as it is a service centre for the local rural area and a popular tourist spot especially over the summer months with the town being located on the northern bank of the Murray River. Land values in the industrial area at Murray Downs have also seen a moderate increase as this area is relatively small and has moderate demand, with the current development suited primary to bulk grain storage and handling.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values overall have shown a moderate increase between July 2023 and July 2024 after a very strong increase in 2022. The movement was not consistent across the local government area. Generally rural values in the hobby farms surrounding Moama of the Murray River Shire showed strong decreases, following larger farmland not selling on the open market due to a rise in interest rates and political changes have seen confidence being affected in a negative manner, with the rural values surround Barham and broader acreage towards the western and northern regions of the shire remaining relatively steady. Murray River Frontage Farms also saw the land values remaining steady.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
