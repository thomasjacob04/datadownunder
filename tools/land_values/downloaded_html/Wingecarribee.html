General overview

Overall, Wingecarribee Local Government Area (LGA) experienced a slight decrease in land values between July 2023 and July 2024, largely due to slight deceases experienced in the residential sector.

Commercial, industrial and rural land values generally remained steady over the same period.

While there remains a steady demand by owner occupiers and investors looking for regional locations close to Sydney, high interest rates, inflationary pressures and increasing construction costs have resulted in a steadying in most market sectors.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values in Wingecarribee Local Government Area (LGA) typically experienced slight decreases between July 2023 and July 2024.

Proximity to Sydney and transport routes, and affordability compared to major cities remain as major drivers of market activity. However, high interest rates, inflationary pressures and increasing construction costs has seen a tempering of market conditions.

Berrima showed moderate to strong increases due to limited supply, whilst larger sites within Exeter showed a strong increase due to their subdivision potential.

Residential allotments with development potential across the LGA have shown moderate increases in value indicating continued demand for residential land despite a general reduction in sales volumes.

Moderate increases were experienced for residential land in Braemar due to expansion of the area with a subdivision known as ‘Nattai Ponds’ estate. The suburb of Willow Vale, being neighbour to Braemar, experienced a strong decrease due to not benefitting from the new subdivision upgrades.

Yerrinbool locality showed slight to moderate increases, with a traditionally low value base placing upward pressure on prices.

Slight increases were shown in Moss Vale and Mittagong medium density zoned properties due to significant redevelopment in the area and good demand for townhouse and villa style developments.

A slight increase was experienced for larger residential properties in Mittagong, due to strong demand and restricted supply for properties within these localities.

New Berrima and Medway experienced strong decreases due to greater supply levels and a less competitive market in this locality.

Both residential and lifestyle zoned properties within Bowral, Robertson, Burradoo, and Braemar have shown strong decreases due to the significant level of investment required and high interest rates, which has lessened demand.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Overall, industrial land values in Wingecarribee Local Government Area (LGA) remained generally steady between July 2023 and July 2024, however this has varied in some instances depending on the locality, environmental factors such as flooding, access and connectivity of infrastructure.

Bowral experienced moderate decreases due to restrictions resulting from recent updated flood mapping, particularly relevant to properties adjoining or adjacent to Mittagong Creek. These restrictions have resulted in increased development, building and insurance costs placing downward pressure on land values.

Moss Vale has shown moderate increase due to recent subdivisions, like Red Fields Road, along with other DA’s being lodged, bringing services to the area and renewing demand for industrial lands in the area.

Wingecarribee LGA proximity to Sydney and transport routes, recent investment and development in the industrial precincts of Braemar and Moss Vale, and the limited supply of vacant light industrial land and warehouse units, have been the major drivers for ongoing market growth within industrial.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, rural land values in Wingecarribee Local Government Area (LGA) remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

The trend was inconsistent across the LGA with value levels impacted by lower sales volumes and limited supply in more tightly held localities.

Smaller rural lots in Yerrinbool Village, Bundanoon and Exeter that enjoyed growth in previous years, experienced strong value decreases between July 2023 and July 2024, with buyers pursuing affordability to counter high interest rates and general cost of living.

In contrast, affordable rural lifestyle and hobby farms in the Southern Highlands around Mittagong, Aylmerton, Bundanoon, Wingello, Berrima and Moss Vale represented good buying by comparison and saw strong value uplifts in the same period.

A key driver of the hobby farm sector is the easy accessibility to Sydney which made Wingecarribee attractive to purchasers.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
