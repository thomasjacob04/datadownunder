 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, residential land values in the Wagga Wagga local government area experienced a slight increase between July 2023 and July 2024. The rise in values can be attributed to limited supply and good demand for residential properties. Wagga Wagga remains affordable to both owner occupiers and investors when compared to the capital cities.
This increase was not consistent across all suburbs with strong increases experienced in Ashmont, Mount Austin, Tolland and Kooringal due to perceived affordability of these locations. A moderate decrease was experienced in Brunslea Park as an adequate supply of properties were brought to market to meet reduced demand.
Very strong increases were seen in the village of Mangoplah and strong increases were experienced in the villages of Collingullie, Uranquinty and Ladysmith with buyers identifying the relative affordability of these locations compared to other villages and the city of Wagga Wagga. Other village land values remained steady after significant increases over recent years.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

This growth level was broadly consistent across all commercial zones and locations except for Enterprise Corridor which experienced a strong increase due to increased demand for property from a combination of outside buyers seeking a return on investment in more affordable regional locations and owner occupiers taking advantage of an uplift in local values.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values in Wagga Wagga experienced a strong increase due to a shortage of available stock throughout the Wagga Wagga industrial areas. There has also been increased buyer demand in the Bowman area with both the Inland Rail development and the Special Activation precinct becoming implemented. This precinct is one of the key growth areas in the city and has attracted both local and metropolitan based buyers.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
