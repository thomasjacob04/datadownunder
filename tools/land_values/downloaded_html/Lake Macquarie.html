General overview

Overall, the Lake Macquarie local government area experienced a slight increase in land values between July 2023 and July 2024.

This upward trend was observed in both the residential and industrial markets, which saw slight increases, while rural land values showed a moderate increase. In contrast, commercial land values showed a slight decrease over the same period.

The market showed signs of improvement, with slight growth recorded following a period of stable values last year. This improvement can largely be attributed to the recent stabilisation of interest rates, along with the anticipation of potential rate cuts.

The market recovery has also been supported by increased demand from buyers migrating from Sydney, drawn by the relative affordability and appealing lifestyle options in the Lake Macquarie region.

Additionally, recent amendments to development controls in Lake Macquarie, aimed at promoting sustainable growth through infill development and boosting housing supply near commercial centres, have likely influenced demand. Changes such as reduced lot sizes, increased building heights, and expanded Medium Density zones are designed to diversify housing options and better integrate residential areas with economic hubs, supporting continued buyer interest and development momentum.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, there was a slight increase in residential land values in the Lake Macquarie local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.

The market showed signs of improvement, with slight growth recorded following a period of stable values last year. This improvement is largely attributed to the recent stabilisation of interest rates, along with the anticipation of potential rate cuts.

Areas that experienced stronger increases include parts of Macquarie Hills, Garden Suburb, Whitebridge, Toronto West, Blackalls Park, Wyee, Belmont, Jewells, Swansea, Caves Beach, Redhead, and englobo entries.

In Murrays Beach/Wallarah Peninsula and the Wyee paper subdivision, un-serviced allotments experienced very strong increases in value. This growth is attributed to speculative purchasing, as buyers and investors are drawn to these properties for their affordability and the possibility of future servicing.

Conversely, parts of Charlestown, Belmont North, Balmoral, Fishing Point, Rathmines, Windermere Park, Morisset Park, Brightwaters, Mirrabooka, Coal Point, Toronto, Kilaben Bay, and waterfront areas in Valentine showed a slight decrease in land values.

The largest (moderate) decrease experienced in Wyee was limited to the Radcliffe Estate, a recently established residential estate on the western boundary of the Wyee township. This reduction in value is attributed to an increase in vacant land within the estate, reduced construction appetite due to significant cost increases, and increased competition from recent releases of vacant land in areas with closer commutable access to Newcastle and Gosford.

Also, Mixed Use land values in Charlestown showed a moderate decrease over this period.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

There was a slight increase to industrial land values in the Lake Macquarie local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.This trend was broadly consistent across most industrial zones.

There has been a very limited release of new industrial land in the wider Hunter Coast region over recent years which has led to a long-term trend of consistently rising land values. The demand for industrial land has also been supported by significant local population growth, particularly in nearby urban release areas such as Morisset and Cooranbong in Lake Macquarie, Raymond Terrace and Medowie in Port Stephens, along with large urban growth areas in the adjoining Maitland and Cessnock local government areas.

More specifically, the developing industrial precincts of Lake Macquarie have experienced good demand and slight value increases. This is due to surrounding population growth and the relative affordability of these precincts which have historically attracted lower prices than other nearby industrial centres positioned adjacent to the M1 Motorway, such as Beresfield and Somersby.

Rural overview

Overall there was a moderate increase in rural land values in the Lake Macquarie local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This trend was broadly consistent across rural zones and locations.

Demand generally comes from locals upgrading to a rural property from a residential property, or migration from one of the adjoining districts or from outer Sydney, due to the relative affordability.

The Deferred Matter zoning in Whitebridge showed steady values given the ongoing uncertainty of the zone and potential uses of this property.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
