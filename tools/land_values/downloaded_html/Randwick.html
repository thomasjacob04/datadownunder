General overview

There was an overall strong increase in land values for the Randwick local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. The strong increase in land values was largely driven by a stabilisation in interest rates, a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year and by greater demand versus limited supply coming to the market. Also, its proximity to a wide range of services and amenities continues to drive this positive trend.

Residential and rural land values increased strongly, whilst commercial and industrial land values showed a moderate increase.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

There was a strong increase in residential land values in the Randwick local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. Whilst we have experienced a stabilisation in interest rates and a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year, the strong increase in land values was largely driven by greater demand versus limited supply coming to the market. The strong increase trend in values can be attributed to the ongoing demand for residential properties in the eastern suburbs including the Randwick local government area. The desirable characteristics include its location close to coastal beaches, commercial centres, regional shopping centres, transport services including the ongoing benefit of the light rail system, private & public schools and the proximity to the Sydney CBD.

The strong increases from July 2023 to July 2024 were relatively consistent across different areas within Randwick local government area. However, medium and high density residential areas across Randwick local government area experienced more moderate increases in values, whilst a small pocket of medium density properties in Kensington and Kingsford remained steady with minimal movement in values. It is characteristic of the Randwick local government area to have variations in value changes, given the large range in residential accommodation and locations.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

There was a moderate increase overall across commercial land values in the Randwick local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. The trend in the commercial land values movement can be attributed to the lack of supply versus demand for well-located properties with development potential and leased properties with secure income streams.

The continuing demand for multi-storey shop-top housing in suburbs such as Maroubra Junction is supporting the positive trend. Also, the commercial precincts continue to be supported by the work from home population.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

There was a moderate increase overall in industrial land values between July 2023 and July 2024. This can be attributed to the reduced supply of industrial land due to properties being rezoned for residential development as well as the overall strong demand for industrial properties in the Greater Sydney Metropolitan industrial real-estate market. The reduced supply of suitable zoned industrial land in Greater Sydney Metropolitan area has had a positive impact on land values for the remaining industrial land within the local government area.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
