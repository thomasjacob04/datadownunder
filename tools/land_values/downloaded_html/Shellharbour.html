Land values in the Shellharbour local government area

South Coast NSW region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Shellharbour area were prepared by Walsh & Monaghan Pty Ltd.

General overview

Overall, Shellharbour Local Government Area land values experienced a slight increase between July 2023 and July 2024.

Generally, residential areas have shown a slight increase compared to 2023 levels, although this varies between locations. Strong increases were seen in rural land values, slight increases in industrial land values, with commercial land remaining steady throughout the period.

While there remains a steady demand by owner occupiers and investors looking for coastal locations within proximity of Sydney, rising inflation and increasing construction costs have resulted in a steady to slight increases across most market sectors.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Values in most of the residential areas have slightly increased compared to 2023 levels, with relatively steady supply and sales activity throughout the period.

Overall, proximity to Sydney and transport routes, and affordability compared to major cities remain as major drivers of market activity.

Sites with subdivision potential have seen a strong increase with interest remaining strong for development land in areas of recent rezonings

Mount Warrigal has experienced strong increases in values due to comparative affordability with other localities.
Strong increases were also seen in waterfront properties between Shellharbour and Warilla, due to the affordability of waterfront properties in these localities compared to Wollongong or Kiama.

Tullimbar residential properties have shown slight decreases, due to good supply in the area. Shellharbour low density properties, and Shellharbour and Shell Cove medium density properties have seen a slight decrease off the back of strong increases with the Marina opening last year.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Values in most of the commercial areas have remained steady from July 2023 through to July 2024, with relatively steady supply and slightly increased sales activity throughout the period.

A limited supply and the tightly held nature of the commercial market has underpinned values in recent years. Local suburban centres continue to experience steady demand by owner occupiers and investors. The market for larger developed properties around the Shellharbour City Centre remains steady, as institutional investors remain cautious following the pandemic.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Values in most industrial areas appear to have shown a slight increase compared to 2023 levels, with reduced sales activity throughout the period.

The exceptions were industrial properties in established estates around Warilla and Barrack Heights which have shown strong increases, as a result of the growth in demand for well serviced, light industrial properties by both owner occupiers and investors.

Shellharbours proximity to Sydney and transport routes, easy access to the port of Port Kembla, and the limited supply of vacant light industrial land and warehouse units, have been the major drivers for ongoing market growth within the industrial sector.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Values in most of the rural areas have shown strong increases compared to 2023 levels, with increased sales activity throughout the period. However, lifestyle properties have shown only a slight increase, inline with the adjoining values in Kiama LGA.

Rural areas mostly comprise rural homesite blocks of varying sizes along the western section of the LGA. While demand for these rural homesites has grown post the pandemic, there is a relatively limited supply which coupled with the tightly held nature of the market has underpinned values.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
