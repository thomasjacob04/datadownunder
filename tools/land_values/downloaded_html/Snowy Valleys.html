Land values in the Snowy Valleys local government area

South East Regional NSW region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Snowy Valleys area were prepared by Opteon Property Group Pty Ltd.

General overview

Overall, the Snowy Valleys local government area remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024. The steady market was attributed to steady local economic growth and steady levels of supply and demand within the market. Perceived affordability from within the district and from out of town kept demand steady throughout the period which largely met supply. High interest rate levels between July 2023 and July 2024 have contributed to the steady market. This was consistent across all sectors.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, the rural sector in the Snowy Valleys local government area remained steady for the 2024 year. This can be attributed to limited demand met by adequate supply of manageable medium sized rural grazing holdings, primarily purchased by graziers and owner occupiers. Higher interest rates have negated good stock prices within this sector, and the absence of anticipated interest rate relief throughout the period has added to the uncertainty for buyers.
This steady market was not consistent across the entirety of this sector. Green timbered properties in the Brindabella locality experienced a very strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024, while green timbered properties across the remainder of the shire experienced a strong increase. This increase was due to a high demand for timbered properties to use for recreational purposes from out of town buyers, met with limited supply.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
