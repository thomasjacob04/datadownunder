General overview

Overall land values in the Cumberland local government area have shown a moderate increase across the local government area from July 2023 and July 2024. This trend was primarily representative of the residential sector, as commercial remained steady and industrial experienced slight increases. The residential sector remained resilient despite inflationary pressures driven by sustained demand and limited supply over the period.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values increased moderately between July 2023 and July 2024. This was consistent and applicable across all residential zoned land, except for low density residential sites in Lidcombe located south of the railway and high density sites remaining relatively steady.

The increase in values can be attributed to an increase in market demand despite inflationary pressures experienced throughout the period. Higher sales activity and constrained stock levels have resulted in increased competition and rising sale prices compared to the prior valuation year. High density development sites were an exception, as the feasibility of these developments are challenged by high construction costs and funding constraints.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Land values of commercial properties remained steady overall between July 2023 and July 2024, except for moderate increases to small retail in the Berala local centre. Another exception were strong decreases to values within the Merrylands town centre, which in prior years were influenced by potential residential uplift due to the precinct's mixed use zoning. Reduced sales volumes and sale prices compared to the prior year supported decreases in land values within this precinct.

Local council planning and investment in several centres is also supporting stabilisation in commercial land values. Examples being projects to improve public domain space by local council. The Cumberland local government area is located at the geographic heart of the Greater Sydney Region, and is well positioned to City of Parramatta Central Business District (CBD), Western Sydney Employment Area and the future Western Sydney Airport.

The productivity support zone fronting Parramatta Road located in the suburbs of Auburn and Lidcombe experienced moderate increases in values. The precinct comprises a mix of commercial uses, with many properties being bulky goods retail centres. Sales evidence on Parramatta Road within the precinct indicated a renewed demand in this location compared to the prior year. Employment and innovation land in Cumberland are well connected with the arterial road network and freight rail network, making it highly accessible to various distribution catchments across the Greater Sydney Region.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Land values of industrial properties increased slightly overall between July 2023 and July 2024. The overall increasing trend was due to continued demand for industrial sites, off the back of strong rental returns and low vacancy rates. The slight increases being further supported by moderate sale volumes across the local government areas many industrial precincts.

Industrial precincts of the Cumberland local government area are well positioned with access to current state significant infrastructure projects. It is also strategically located between the developing Western Sydney airport and Parramatta CBD with many precincts having direct access to major transport links like the M4 Motorway and Cumberland Highway.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
