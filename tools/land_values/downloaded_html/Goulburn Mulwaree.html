 Residential land values

Residential overview

The residential sector for the Goulburn Mulwaree local government area experienced a slight increase between July 2023 and July 2024. Market drivers supporting this increase include an increased supply of vacant land in the city of Goulburn and the village of Marulan, as well as the ongoing local economic growth which was driven by construction.
This increase was not consistent across the sectors, with rural residential land, general residential land and low density residential englobo sites in Marulan experiencing a strong increase. This increase was attributed to an increased supply and demand of vacant land within the village of Marulan. Central, west, north and flat and unit sites located in Goulburn along with low density residential englobo sites also experienced a strong increase, this was due to high demand for these types of properties from both local and out of town buyers.
Mixed use residential englobo sites and large lot properties with subdivision potential experienced a very strong increase for the 2024 year, which can be attributed to the strong demand within the Goulburn city for vacant land.
Rural residential lands in Tallong experienced a strong decrease, while residential land in the village of Tallong experienced a strong decrease. This decrease can be attributed to is due to the lack of essential services such as reliable transportation, healthcare, and education as well as the absence of infrastructure within the villages, resulting in buyers wishing to live closer to major centres.
The village of Bungonia experienced a very strong increase due to the appeal to live in a rural style village and the close proximity to the National Park, however, still be within a close proximity to major centres such as Canberra and Sydney.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, the rural sector in the Goulburn Mulwaree local government area experienced a moderate increase between July 2023 and July 2024. This increase can be attributed to the increase of livestock prices as well as ideal seasonal conditions which resulted in landowners receiving higher crop yields than previous years. Increased employment opportunities and local business growth are still market drivers within this sector. This increase was not consistent across this sector.
Small and medium rural landscape hobby farms along with rough bush retreats also remained steady due to adequate supply to meet demand from both local and out of town buyers. Flood plain lands and hobby farms with no subdivision potential also remained steady due to buyers looking to purchase land suitable for subdivision.
Primary production zoned small hobby farms experienced a very strong increase for the 2024 base year. This increase in values can be attributed to good demand for this type of property from both lifestyle buyers and local farmers looking to expand areas with smaller build up blocks.
Rural Landscape zoned large hobby farms experienced a moderate decrease for the 2024 base year. This was due to land owners holding on to their properties for a longer period, causing limited supply of these types of properties.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
