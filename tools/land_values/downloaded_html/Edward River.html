General overview

Land values in the Edward River local government remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024. The steady trend in land values was largely being driven by the rural sector, rural values which comprise almost 77% of the total land value of the local government area. The steady trend in land values was not consistent across all market segments, with residential and industrial values having a slight increase.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall have shown slight increases. The majority of residential land in the local government area is within the regional township of Deniliquin that provides goods and services to the wider rural community. With purchasers looking for more affordable housing options, regional towns are proving to be increasingly attractive to purchasers which is supporting demand. The increase was not consistent across all market segments in the local government area. There is generally a market preference for established homes that do not require any immediate upgrades or renovation due to increasing building costs and relatively long waiting times for builders.
The small villages of Blighty, Conargo and Wanganella have shown only slight increases as these villages are constrained by limited services. However, Billabong Estate, Mulumbah and Pretty Pine have seen a slight decrease.
Higher value Murray River frontage parcels in Deniliquin however showed moderate decreases. Whilst small residential lots off Davidson Street have seen moderate increases. A small part of the North Deniliquin residential properties has seen a slight decrease in land values which aligns with the movements from previous years and aligns with neighbouring areas. Rising interest rates and delays in building construction have tempered the market. Purchasers are still attracted to recreational activities available as well as the local services and amenities and the lifestyle Deniliquin and Villages provide.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial values overall have remained steady. Demand for commercial properties has been consistent with demand meeting supply from both local and out of town buyers underpinned by the solid rural outlook and confidence in the region. Throughout the north Deniliquin enterprise corridor, land values have remained steady, providing consistency to small business operators within the Deniliquin township to continue operating in the area.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values have slightly increased overall. Purchasers are primarily local investors and owner occupiers who operate businesses in the Deniliquin area. Demand has generally increased and development continuing. The main drivers for the local economy are agriculture, food production and value-adding, as well as transportation linkages given Deniliquin's position along major transport networks.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
