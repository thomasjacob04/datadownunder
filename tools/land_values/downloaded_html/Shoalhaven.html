General overview

Overall, the Shoalhaven local government area experienced a slight decrease between July 2023 to July 2024.

Residential areas have shown a slight decrease in movement from 2023 levels, however, the amount has varied between locations. Industrial values have experienced strong increases, while commercial values remained steady and rural values have experienced a moderate decrease.

Steady demand by owner occupiers and investors looking for coastal locations close to Sydney, combined with interest rate rises, rising inflation and increasing construction costs have affected land values across many market sectors.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Values in most of the residential areas have shown a slight decrease compared to 2023 levels.

Overall, the local market's main drivers continue to be proximity to Sydney and Wollongong, a strong retiree market and appeal of coastal lifestyle, but higher interest rates, higher inflation and increasing construction costs has seen a tempering of market conditions.

Strong decreases were seen in waterfront and waterfront reserve, properties at Orient Point, Sanctuary Point, Berrara, Cudmirrah, Burrill Lake and Myola, as well as waterfront and canal waterfront in the villages of Conjola Park and Lake Conjola. Villages at Lake Tabourie, and Fisherman’s Paradise also experienced strong decreases. These decreases are generally a result of the increasing costs to build on flood liable land and holding costs, combined with the reduction in demand in the holiday house market.

Ocean front and ocean view residential properties around Ulladulla and Mollymook have also shown strong decreases. These areas are traditionally an investor and holiday market. Demand has softened from this sector with owner occupiers now driving the market and affected by reduced affordability

Medium density land in Bomaderry, Sanctuary Point, Huskisson and Callala also experienced strong decreases, with the medium density market being impacted by the high cost of building

Bomaderry has also shown a strong decrease with demand affected by broader economic factors.

Berry land values and larger residential sites in Basin View have also shown strong decreases with significant level of investment required and increased cost associated with borrowing has lessened demand.

Slight increases were shown in Vincentia and Basin View, medium density residential in Sussex Inlet, larger sites in Ulladulla and Depot Beach due to strong demand with a very reduced market supply.

Nowra has shown slight to moderate increases following increased demand due to relative affordability.

Lifestyle properties north of Shoalhaven River have also shown slight increases properties south of Shoalhaven River have shown a moderate increase. These pockets are experiencing high value activity with limited supply and strong demand.

Greenwell Point waterfront properties have shown slight increases, while absolute waterfront properties have shown strong increases. Moderate increases were experienced in Huskisson absolute waterfront and Manyana waterfront, due to strong demand and limited supply.

Hyams Beach has shown moderate increases whilst the waterfront properties have shown very strong increases. Similarly, properties in Culburra have shown moderate increases while beach front marina lots have shown very strong increases, due to strong demand and restricted supply.

Callala Bay has shown a moderate increase, with Callala Beach experiencing strong increases. A tightly held location with relatively restricted supply has supported value movements.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Values in the commercial areas have remained steady from July 2023 through to July 2024 with steady sales volumes throughout the period.

The demand from owner occupiers and investors is contributing to the tightening supply within the commercial market sector.

The exceptions include Berry and Culburra, which experienced moderate increases over the period. These are generally located in smaller village locations heavily reliant on tourism. Commercial values in Ulladulla and some villages experienced moderate to strong increases on the back of emerging developments in prominent locations.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Values in most industrial areas have experienced strong increases from July 2023 through to July 2024, with relatively steady sales activity throughout the period.

Increased demand from both owner occupiers and investors is contributing to the tightening of supply which has led to the increases in land values.

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
