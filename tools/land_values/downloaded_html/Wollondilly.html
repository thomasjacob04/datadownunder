General overview

Land values overall across the Wollondilly local government area between July 2023 and July 2024 have remained steady, with this trend consistent across residential and rural markets.

High interest rates, inflation and increased construction costs, have affected values within Wollondilly LGA.

Commercial properties showed a moderate increase and industrial showed a strong increase with demand driven by owner occupiers and investors supporting land values.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values remained steady overall between July 2023 and July 2024, however this has varied between locations.

Demand is driven by relative affordability and continued population growth, as met by the New Wilton Development plan. The impact of the 2019-2020 bushfires across the LGA has lessened, however buyer caution remains in some bushfire prone locations.

Lifestyle properties at Bargo, Tahmoor and Weronmba, showed moderate to strong decreases due to the longer-term impact of the 2019 - 2020 bushfires in these areas.

Residential properties within Oakdale and Nattai experienced moderate to strong decreases due to oversupply resulting from new subdivisions in Oakdale.

Buxton, Camden Park, and residential and lifestyle properties in Belimba Park showed moderate decreases. The purchasers in this area are more circumspect in consideration of broader economic factors.

Moderate increases were experienced in Bargo lifestyle properties, Silverdale, Douglas Park, and Appin medium density residential, whilst Menangle properties experienced strong increases. These localities are areas of recent subdivisions which is driving these increases.

Village properties in Wollondilly have moderately increased, which is in line with the Industrial market, as is the nature of the properties within this zoning.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Values in most of the industrial areas have shown strong increases over 2023 levels, although the movement varied across localities.

Industrial properties in Picton and Thirlmere showed a strong decrease, with demand for industrial properties in the area easing, and interest shifting to the northern villages.

Warragamba showed a very strong increase due to the proximity to the new Badgerys Creek airport, with investors and developers seeking to acquire quality assets and large land holdings.

Generally, there has been an increasing demand for industrial land in Wollondilly LGA, due to the close proximity to major south-western Sydney infrastructure projects, including the development of the new Airport and Aerotropolis, therefore attracting interest from investors and developers.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Values in most of the rural areas have remained steady from July 2023 through to July 2024, however, the increases varied between localities.

The rural sector in Wollondilly is a popular market with many sales being driven by affordability in comparison to larger more populated metropolitan areas and lifestyle choices. Given the increasing rural residential nature of rural zoned properties, land values in the rural sector are influenced to a large extent by residential market conditions.

Rural lands to the western portion of the LGA, within the localities of Yanderra, Pheasants Nest , Buxton and Bargo, The Oaks, Oakdale and Belimbla Park, together with villages west of Thirlmere and Picton, experienced moderate to strong decreases. This is a result of the longer-term impact of the 2019 - 2020 bushfires in these areas.

Strong decreases were also shown in rural residential lots within Oakdale, which directly correlates to the decrease of values within Oakdale residential property market. Moderate decreases were shown in smaller rural lots centrally positioned within the LGA, within the localities of Appin, Douglas Park, Wilton, The Oaks, Razorback, Picton, Mount Hunter, Glenmore, Cawdor and Brownlow Hill, following the residential market trends.

Smaller rural sized properties within Douglas Park experienced strong increases, in line with Douglas Park residential moderate increase.

Strong increases were also experienced within the Wilton growth area, due to encroaching residential development. Larger rural lands in the adjoining villages of Wilton, Appin and Picton also experienced the same level of increase.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
