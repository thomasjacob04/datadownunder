General overview

There was a slight increase in land values overall in Inner West local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. Increases in land values was not consistent across all markets segments with residential properties showing a moderate increase, industrial properties showing a slight increase, and commercial properties showing a slight decrease.

The primary contributors to the overall increase were the residential and industrial sectors, which benefited from sustained demand and market interest.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall residential land values in the Inner West local government area showed a moderate increase between July 2023 and July 2024. However, these increases were not consistent across all residential localities.

The majority of mixed use precincts within the district were an exception to the overall trend and generally remained steady, except for properties to the Camperdown Health & Education precinct in Stanmore fronting Parramatta Rd, that moderately decreased more in line with the trend to the adjacent local centre strip retail.

Other areas that remained stable were within the medium and low density residential zones comprising of low scale unit sites and single residential waterfront properties. However, low scale unit stock within the general residential zones in the suburbs of Annandale, Balmain, Balmain East, Birchgrove, Leichhardt, Lilyfield, and Rozelle experienced slight decreases.

Additionally, the many existing retail shops within the general residential zone in the same suburbs of Annandale, Balmain, Balmain East, Birchgrove, Leichhardt, Lilyfield, and Rozelle experienced moderate decreases which was more reflective of the trend for properties in the commercial zone, and due to subdued demand reflected by sales evidence within those localities compared to the prior year.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

There was an overall slight decrease in commercial land values. Sales throughout the district have overall supported this trend.

However, properties zoned commercial town centre in Ashfield experienced strong decreases in land values. This decline was partly attributed to ongoing challenges in the retail sector, including a continued trend of negative real growth in retail turnover, along with stricter lending criteria for businesses, particularly in retail.

In contrast, properties zoned local centre in Ashfield, Croydon, Haberfield, and Summer Hill saw a slight increase in land values. These areas, which include low-rise retail strip shops, business/commercial premises, and mixed-use developments, serve the surrounding residential communities. The main drivers of this increase appear to be steady local consumer demand and a limited supply of commercial properties in these tightly zoned areas. This combination of demand from nearby residents and restricted availability of space has contributed to a modest rise in values within these community-focused commercial zones.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Overall, industrial land values in the Inner West local government area experienced a slight increase from July 2023 to July 2024. However, there was a notable exception in the business development single residential zoned sites in St Peters, where land values saw a more moderate rise.

This trend has been driven by ongoing gentrification of industrial land which is reducing the number of available industrial properties, whilst overall demand has generally remained strong. The improved road network around the St Peters interchange and proximity to the Sydney CBD, Port Botany and Sydney airport has increased the demand for these sites.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
