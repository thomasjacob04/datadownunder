 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, residential land values remained steady in the Kyogle LGA between July 2023 and July 2024, which is consistent to other locations on the Far North Coast.

The exceptions were flood prone properties impacted by the 2022 floods, where values have experienced a moderate decrease.

In contrast, values increased moderately in Cawongla and Wiangaree villages and there have been strong increases in Old Bonalbo and Tabulam villages. This is primarily due to affordability, proximity to the Queensland border and the growing popularity for a village lifestyle.

Market drivers continue to be affordability throughout the majority of the LGA with value levels in Kyogle being lower than most other locations on the North Coast. In non-flood prone locations demand has been experienced from purchasers relocating from other North Coast locations.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Overall, commercial land values in the Kyogle LGA experienced a slight increase from July 2023 to July 2024.

The Kyogle commercial sector is small and thinly traded, with stable demand levels for limited supply.

The larger nearby centres of Casino and Lismore limit growth in the Kyogle commercial centre with the smaller population base providing limited demand. The market is also influenced by relative affordability compared to other North Coast localities.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Overall, industrial land values in the Kyogle LGA were steady from July 2023 to July 2024.

Flood free industrial land in other North Coast locations have continued to increase in recent years, which has contained growth for flood liable localities. The more affordable entry to the Kyogle industrial sector remains attractive to the market.

The market is a thinly traded primary owner occupier market with modest demand for a very limited supply.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, rural land values in the Kyogle LGA showed a slight increase from July 2023 to July 2024. Moderate to strong increases for rural lifestyle properties and more affordable hobby farms and sites west of Richmond Range were noted, driven by demand from buyers seeking more affordable options.

Demand for Primary Production land was led by interest from western, interstate, and local graziers seeking quality grazing and cropping land and more relatively reliable water sources and showed a strong increase.

Some isolated and remote pockets showed a moderate decrease due to oversupply and slowing market conditions.

Lifestyle choices as well as longer term confidence in the agricultural sector also remain key drivers in the Kyogle rural sector.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
