Land values in the Blacktown local government area

Sydney Central West region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Blacktown area were prepared by Crown Valuation Service.

General overview

Overall, there was a strong increase in land values in the Blacktown local government area (LGA) between July 2023 to July 2024. The commercial zoned land has exhibited a moderate increase and there has been a slight increase in industrial zoned land. Properties in these localities continue to benefit from the growth of the Northwest Sydney region which continues to see investment in infrastructure and includes areas of significant redevelopment.
There was a very strong decrease in rural zoned land values in this location. The key factor that has influenced the downward value movement in this location has been planning updates regarding the future development potential in this region following several major flooding events across Western Sydney in recent years. The recommendations were addressed in the publications of the State-led rezoning Finalisation Reports for Marsden Park North and West Schofields Precinct, dated August 2023. The planning departments response was that due to potential significant increases to flood related risk to life and property, it would not be prudent to continue with the planned rezonings of certain precincts.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values have shown a strong increase within the Blacktown LGA between July 2023 to July 2024. This movement in land values was relatively consistent across all residential market segments.
Medium and high density residential zoned sites as well as, mixed use land within Blacktown LGA have shown steady land values between July 2023 to July 2024. This is due to the market's continued preference for lower risk, lower scale residential development in this location such as single residential dwellings and dual occupancies. There are several localities within Blacktown LGA that continue to see demand for redevelopment as part of the on-going growth of the region. The environmental living sites in the district showed a moderate decrease following several major flooding events across Western Sydney in recent years.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial land values in the Blacktown LGA exhibited a moderate increase from July 2023 to July 2024. This was not consistent across all commercial properties. There were steady land values in the Neighbourhood and Local Centre, Business Development, Enterprise Corridor and Productivity Support lands. Many of these properties are smaller and are located in more established areas which have not experienced the same value uplift over this period.
Very strong increases were seen in Business Park, Employment and Commercial Centre zoned lands. The properties are typically located in developing areas such as Marsden Park and Ropes Crossing. Part of this value increase is reflective of the roll out of services and market expectations of the upcoming ability to develop these lands.
Commercial land in the Blacktown LGA benefits from good proximity to transport nodes, including the M4 and M7 Motorway and the new Western Sydney Airport. It is well located to capitalise on infrastructure such as the Sydney Metro Northwest to Cudgegong Road Station, and a public transport corridor extension towards Marsden Park.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values have shown a slight increase within the Blacktown LGA between July 2023 to July 2024. This movement in land values was relatively consistent across all the various industrial market segments.
There was a strong decrease in the largely unserviced, industrial zoned land located in Vineyard and Riverstone. The properties are made up of small undeveloped sites which cannot currently be developed. The risk and potential timeline in this area being serviced and developed has been the primary driver for the strong decrease.
Access to the M7 and M4 motorways, proximity to the proposed Western Sydney Airport and a growing distribution and logistics property market are among the primary drivers for value increases. Land for industrial development continues to benefit from robust demand from both owner occupiers and developers with supply constrained.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
