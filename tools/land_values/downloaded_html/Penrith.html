Land values experienced strong increases overall in the Penrith local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This is not consistent across all market segments. There has been a slight increase in rural zoned properties, and strong increases in residential, commercial and industrial properties.

The general upward trend can be attributed to the current and future benefits associated with the construction of the Western Sydney Airport and the Western Sydney Airport Metro Line, such as improving connectivity, increase employment opportunities and improved infrastructure. Strong population growth throughout Western Sydney is also contributing to strong demand for all types of property.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall residential land values in the Penrith local government area showed a strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024. The general upward trend can be attributed to the current and future benefits associated with the construction of the Western Sydney Airport and the Western Sydney Airport Metro Line, such as improving connectivity, increase employment opportunities and improved infrastructure. Strong population growth throughout Western Sydney is also contributing to this strong demand.

Mixed use zoned and high density properties have shown slight decreases. These lower increases relative to the rest of the residential zones is due to the rapid increases properties in these zones have experienced over the previous few years affecting their affordability, and also suffering from an oversupply of apartments.
Low density zoned properties in the River Road Emu Plains component only saw a slight increase, likely due to their higher price point relative to other areas in the district, due to their prominent location on the river.
Environmental living properties south of the M4 in Glenmore Park and Mulgoa were another exception, experiencing a moderate decrease in values. This is due to their relatively remote location and proximity to amenities.
Urban zoned density properties in Jordan Springs only saw slight increases, while englobo properties in Jordan Springs remained steady. This is a result of continued concerns over subsidence issues in the area.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Overall commercial land values in the Penrith local government area saw strong increases between July 2023 and July 2024. The general upward trend can be attributed to the current and future benefits associated with the construction of the Western Sydney Airport and the Western Sydney Airport Metro Line, such as improving connectivity, increase employment opportunities and improved infrastructure and the continued rise of Penrith as a major CBD. Strong population growth throughout Western Sydney is also contributing to strong demand for commercial property.

Agribusiness and enterprise properties experienced slight increases with commercial centre properties experienced moderate decreases, and Mixed Use properties remained steady. The moderate decreases for commercial centre properties overall were driven by a small pocket of properties being decreased in line with sales evidence, and as a result of the large increases experienced in 2023.

The agribusiness areas of the Western Sydney Aerotropolis have only seen slight increases due to the uncertainty around future land uses for properties in the Luddenham Village and long lead times for future developments, while there are only 3 Enterprise properties in the district and have been valued on the conservative side to the limited sales evidence for the zone.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values have experienced strong increases from July 2023 to July 2024, due to strong demand for industrial sites by owner-occupiers and institutional investors, particularly for large sites (over 1-hectare) for future redevelopment, or use within the strong performing areas of logistics, e-commerce, and growing demand for large data centres. The strong increases have been supported by robust sales volumes across the districts many industrial precincts.

General industrial properties in Erskine Park experienced very strong increases, while properties around Kemps Creek remained steady. The Erskine Park area is set to benefit from the opening of the Western Sydney International Airport, with the added benefit of already being fully serviced, and not facing the development issues that some of the recently rezoned industrial areas surrounding the airport are facing such as Kemps Creek and the stormwater issues occurring there.

General industrial properties in Emu Plains also experienced a moderate decrease. This is due to greater interest in other industrial precincts in the district, with the other industrial precincts seeing greater benefits from infrastructure investment in the region.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall rural land values in the Penrith local government area experienced slight increases between July 2023 and July 2024. This was not consistent across all zones, with rural landscape zoned properties, primary production properties in Wallacia, and primary production small lots properties in Cranebrook, Londonderry, Llandilo, Regentville and Castlereagh/Agnes Banks seeing strong increases. Primary production small lots in Orchard Hills on the other hand saw strong decreases.

The general upward trend can be attributed to the current and future benefits associated with the construction of the Western Sydney Airport and the Western Sydney Airport Metro Line, such as improving connectivity, increase employment opportunities and improved infrastructure. Strong population growth throughout Western Sydney is also contributing to strong demand for rural property.

Orchard Hills primary production small lot properties have experienced strong decreases with the exception of properties in the immediate vicinity of the Orchard Hills Metro Station currently under construction. Properties closer to the metro are more desirable to investors due to their development potential, with the rest of the component not seeing as much interest from buyers.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
