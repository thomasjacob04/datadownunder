Sydney South West region

General overview

Between July 2023 and July 2024, land values in the Camden local government area (LGA) experienced a slight increase overall. However, this trend was not consistent across all market segments. Commercial and Industrial zones saw a strong increase, attributed to ongoing infrastructure improvements and the area's relative affordability compared to other regions closer to the Sydney CBD. Conversely, rural properties showed a moderate decrease, due to a move away from the COVID induced tree change and a shift towards a return to working from the office and ongoing flooding issues identified in the area.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values in the Camden LGA showed a slight increase overall between July 2023 and July 2024, although this trend varied across different zones and suburbs.

In the Camden Centre, mixed use properties that are not flood prone showed a slight decrease. Mixed use zoned land in Leppington and Gledswood Hill that are identified within future redevelopment precincts showed a strong increase. Low density zoned land in Currans Hill also showed a strong increase driven by its desirable location and surrounding amenities. Flood-affected sites (50% or more) in Camden, Camden South and Elderslie showed a slight decrease whilst low density sites on the eastern side of Old Hume Highway and Broughton Street in Camden showed a strong decrease due to its proximity to flood prone land.

Land values of residentially zoned recreational open space saw strong increases throughout the LGA. Medium density land in Leppington showed a slight decrease overall, while larger medium density lots in the LGA saw strong increases. Englobo properties within the Camden LGA exhibited strong to very strong value increases from July 2023 to July 2024. This is largely attributed to the release of new areas and the development of adjacent services, enhancing the appeal and potential of these properties for future development. Strong demand for larger properties with the potential for further development has led to increases in land values.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
