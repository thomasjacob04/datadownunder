 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall have shown a moderate increase in values between July 2023 and July 2024. The urban growth corridor centred in Thurgoona continues to be popular with purchasers looking for newer, more modern homes. The established areas of Lavington and Norris Park and several predominantly social housing areas all showed moderate increases as a larger number of purchasers are looking for more affordable housing options. The more central and traditionally higher valued established areas in Albury including some mixed use zoned areas have remained steady as have some of the higher valued, larger lifestyle parcels at Doctors Point, Table Top and Splitters Creek. The land values within the village of Lake Hume, comprising predominately holiday accommodation, have also remained steady, with village zoned parcels at Ettamogah, in proximity to the Hume Highway corridor showing a moderate decrease. Low density residential properties in Lavington and West Albury also generally remained steady. The values of residential englobo property located in the Thurgoona Urban Release area have shown a very strong increase as developers “land bank” sites for future residential subdivision.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial land values overall have shown a slight increase with prime, well tenanted CBD properties generally being tightly held. Land values in the prime commercial centre of central Albury have remained steady, with minimal sales transacted and steady rental income also having an effect on the values throughout the 2023/24 period. Local, interstate and national investors continue to show confidence in the region and recognise Albury City’s strong growth rate, solid employment base and strategic location on the Sydney-Melbourne transport corridor. A small number of productivity support and enterprise zoned properties in Ettamogah have shown a strong increase, as these are generally larger parcels suited to industrial development with good access to both northbound and southbound lanes of the Hume Freeway via the Davey Road interchange.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values have shown a strong increase with demand for industrial properties increasing as the Albury Wodonga economy continues to expand. There has been strong demand from both investors and owner occupiers acquiring existing premises within the established industrial estates in addition to vacant land purchases to develop purpose-built facilities. The expansion of the Airside North Industrial Estate in particular on Ceres Drive in Thurgoona highlights the confidence that private developers have in expanding the supply to meet ongoing and future industrial demand.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values have shown a moderate increase between July 2023 and July 2024. The majority of rural properties in the Albury local government area are below 100 hectares and generally considered below the size required for a standalone commercial primary production enterprise. They are generally larger rural residential and add on grazing properties predominantly owned by professionals who operate businesses in Albury and Wodonga. The accessibility and proximity to Albury and Wodonga providing for a broad range of goods and services underpins demand.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
