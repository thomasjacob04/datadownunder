 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

General residential zoned properties located in the newer precinct of Settlement Shores with canal and river frontage experienced a moderate increase in values, due to good demand from local home buyers, tree changers and investors.

Medium density residential zoned properties in the Port Macquarie corridor extending to the east experienced a strong decrease in values, due to more subdued demand following two consecutive years of moderate and very strong increases.

High Density zoned properties in Port Macquarie experienced a moderate decrease in values, due to more subdued demand following two consecutive years of moderate and very strong increases.

Elevated building costs are impacting viability for new density developments, with increased stock levels available for existing readily built villas, townhouses and units.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values overall remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

An exception to this trend includes the Port Macquarie general industrial area, comprising of properties located to the north and south of Lake Road, which experienced a moderate decrease in land values. This was due to the underlying supply in stock in the area and weakening demand.

The Wauchope general industrial area experienced a strong increase in land values, due to the emerging nature and growth shown by readily developable land in this area.

Also, the Herons Creek heavy industrial area experienced a strong increase in land values, due to strong demand from the forestry industry for Australian hardwood and softwood products, together with undersupply of land stock.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values overall remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

Rural properties located in the Le Clos Verdun Estate experienced a strong decrease in value due to lessening demand and uncertainties about future potential/dwelling eligibility of lots in this estate.

Rural residential and grazing properties in Telegraph Point to Rollands Plains and Comboyne experienced a moderate decline due to reduced demand in these locations during 2024. Increased availability of rural lifestyle properties closer to larger towns and services has increased options available to buyers. Dry homesite lots in North Shore also experienced a moderate decrease due to a reduction in demand for homesite lots that are only accessible via ferry services, and plentiful supply of vacant land in Port Macquarie.

Rural bushland and grazing properties west of Wauchope experienced a strong increase in value. This aligns with a body of sales indicating strong demand particularly for larger sites with some having river frontage. Demand has been from ‘out of town’ purchasers and locals alike. Supply is restricted for this type of real estate which has been driving values upward for the 1 July 2024 base date.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
