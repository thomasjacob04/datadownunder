Sydney Coast South region

General overview

There was an overall moderate increase in land values for the Bayside local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This trend was generally consistent across the residential, commercial, industrial and rural market segments.

The moderate increase in land values within the local government area was largely driven by a stabilisation in interest rates and a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year.

Another significant local driver was the lack of supply which propped up demand for properties due to their desirable location and close proximity to coastal beaches, public transport infrastructure and Sydney CBD.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

There was a moderate increase in residential land values in the Bayside local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This trend can be attributed to the lack of supply and ongoing demand for residential properties in the southern Sydney metropolitan region within the Bayside local government area due to its desirable location, being close to coastal beaches, commercial centres, transport services and the Sydney CBD.

The general moderate increase in land values within the local government area is also driven by a stabilisation in interest rates and a strengthening of market sentiment over the past year.

This trend was not consistent across the entire residential sector. Properties with a Medium Density zone in localities of Hillsdale, Daceyville and Pagewood as well as Low Density Residential zoned properties in various locations, showed steady changes in land values, whilst some high density residential properties in Eastgardens showed very strong increases in land value. It is a characteristic of Bayside local government area to have variations in value changes, given its large variations in residential accommodation and locations.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
