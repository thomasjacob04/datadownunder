General overview

Overall, Tweed local government area experienced a slight increase in land value levels between July 2023 and July 2024.

This trend was consistent across residential and commercial values, with strong increases in the industrial sector and moderate increases in rural land values.

Tweed continues to benefit from improved transportation links following completion of the Pacific Highway upgrade and ready access to the nearby Gold Coast airport. Other market drivers include the temperate climate and various available coastal and hinterland lifestyle choices.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, residential values within the Tweed local government area experienced a slight increase between July 2023 and July 2024.

Strong value increases was evident in larger medium density residential land at Kingscliff, Casuarina, Banora Point, Murwillumbah West and the villages of Chillingham, Tyalgum and Uki. Similarly, sustained demand for canal lots, Banora river-front and large lot and development land also saw values increase strongly in these residential market sectors.

Strong value increases were evident in Kings Forest greenfield sites as new subdivisions came online creating confidence in that market. Elsewhere, moderate decreases were generally experienced by development lands across the LGA with rising construction costs affecting demand from developers

Residential land in the suburbs of Terranora and Bilambil Heights also experienced a slight value decrease along with larger density sites across the LGA.

Tumbulgum Village values remained steady as supply balanced demand. Easing demand for Terranora Village resulted in moderate value decreases.

Good access following the Pacific Highway upgrade and ready access to the nearby Gold Coast airport are key drivers for this market. Other drivers include the temperate climate and various coastal and hinterland lifestyle choices available in the region making it attractive to people looking to move from the larger metropolitan areas.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, rural land values in the Tweed local government experienced moderate increases between July 2023 and July 2024.

Values of larger rural properties in the more remote western areas remained steady, as supply continued to meet demand in these locations.

A very strong increase was noted for the tightly held and agriculturally intensive red soil country around Cudgen and Duranbah. Good seasonal conditions and favourable commodity prices, combined with proximity to key amenities such as the Tweed Valley Hospital, airport, and beaches, continue to underpin land values in these lifestyle localities.

Rising construction costs led to reduced demand for development sites, causing a slight decrease in the value of rural land with tourist or development potential.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
