 Residential land values

Residential overview

Parkes residential values remained steady in value from July 2023 to July 2024.
The properties in east Parkes experienced a moderate increase in values, followed by a strong increase in the Shallow Rush area. Driving factors for this increase came from both the investor and homeowner markets. The North Parkes Mine, Parkes National Logistic Hub, Parkes Bypass and the Special Activation Precinct have continued to provide solid market drivers and create employment opportunities across the district. The impact resulting in upward pressure on the housing market.
Peak Hill Village experienced a moderate increase in value due to the expansion of Tomingley Mine. Bogan Gate and Trundle Village experienced a very strong increase in values, due to the higher demand for low value blocks in small villages generally due to their relative affordability. These are sometimes in the form of speculative purchases of vacant sites, with the potential opening of Fifield Mine encouraging buyers in the region.
Large lot residential with a minimum lot size of 4 hectares have seen a moderate decrease in values due to high interest rates and cost of living, decreasing the demand for more expensive, larger allotments on the fringe of town.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values across the Parkes local government area experienced steady values from July 2023 to July 2024.
High interest rate, as well as commodity price fluctuation and dry seasonal conditions in the region have resulted in a more recent slowing of the activity in this market. Purchasers remain predominantly locals looking to expand their current holdings for succession purposes and increasing economies of scale.
There were some exceptions to this with mixed farming in the Trundle/Bogan Gate district seeing a moderate decrease in values with this location having a higher than usual supply of properties for sale and limited demand.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
