 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values in Bourke experienced a strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024.

This increase was the result of North Bourke showing very strong increases between 2023 and 2024 due to continued demand with North Bourke being considered a more desirable location due to its lifestyle potential.

The remaining village values have remained steady in line with residential values in the town of Bourke.The limited increase in Bourke and the villages was a result of the remote location, increased cost of construction and the limited population not generating the demand to see value increases.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values within Bourke saw strong increases between July 2023 and July 2024. The strong overall increase in industrial values was mainly attributed to the industrial land at North Bourke being utilised as residential use with strong demand evident for residential parcels located outside the Bourke township with North Bourke being considered a more desirable location due to its lifestyle potential. Steady market conditions for industrial parcels within the Bourke township reflect the remoteness of the location and the high establishment costs to invest in or establish industrial activities in the region.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural values in Bourke remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024 as supply was met with demand. Generally, the rural market in western New South Wales had been in a period of strong value growth however at the end of 2023 an extended dry period and low stock prices slowed the market considerably. The grazing market appears to remain subdued with some agents reporting that rural finance is more difficult to achieve mainly due to the decreased returns and higher interest rates. However, smaller rural parcels near Bourke saw strong increases between 2023 and 2024 given a strong influence from the residential market within the township of Bourke.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
