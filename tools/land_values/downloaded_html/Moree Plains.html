 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, residential land values across the Moree Plains local government area experienced an moderate increase in values between July 2023 and July 2024.

Low density residential at Greenbah and Amaroo, environmental living and residential at Mungindi, Boomi and Boggabilla remained steady, reflecting decreased activity since completion of the Inland Rail Project, coupled with high inflation and interest rate rises.

Conversely, the generally better located village markets of Ashley, Gurley, Biniguy, Garah and Weemelah experienced very strong increases due to increased appeal of these more affordable locations. The more affordable central area of Moree experienced very strong value increases.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values across the Moree Plains local government area experienced an overall slight increase in values between July 2023 and July 2024.

A continuation of about average seasonal conditions, on-going uncertainty around interest rates and a generally positive outlook for rural commodity prices, contributed to the present market conditions.

Quality broadacre properties to the east of Moree experienced a strong increase with further increases evident for good quality land in well-regarded, tightly held areas.

A small cluster of grazing properties to the southeast of Moree, and running onto Mount Kaputar National Park, experienced very strong value increases.

Larger lifestyle properties, well located to Moree, also experienced very strong increases driven by strong demand for rural lands over the last several years, coupled with the limited availability of these desirable properties.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
