General overview

Overall, there was a strong increase in land values in the Northern Beaches local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. The strong increase in land values was primarily driven by the strengthening demand for residential properties in the Northern Beaches especially in the latter half of 2023 to early 2024.

There was a slight increase in commercial land values across the local government area between July 2023 and July 2024 with continued demand for commercial properties. There was also a strong increase in industrial and rural land values during the same period.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

There was a strong increase overall in residential land values in the Northern Beaches local government area between 2023 and 2024. Whilst market movement trended in a positive direction for all areas, due to the diversity of residential properties in the Northern Beaches, this increase has not been consistent. Residential properties range from standard, inland dwellings to prestige, beach and ocean front properties. Demand for a particular type of property fluctuates as evidenced by market sentiment.
Environmental Living zoned locations including Palm Beach, Elanora Heights, North Narrabeen, Warriewood and Mona Vale, have evidenced steady to slight increases in land values.
Low Density Residential zoned locations including Avalon Beach, Elanora Heights, North Narrabeen, parts of Narrabeen, Collaroy, Collaroy Plateau, Mona Vale and Warriewood have evidenced steady to slight increases in land values.
Medium Density zoned sites in locations including parts of Dee Why, Freshwater, Manly Vale, Mona Vale, Narrabeen, North Narrabeen and Warriewood have experienced slight increases in land values.
Mixed Use zoned sites in locations such as Dee Why and Mona Vale, have experienced steady land values over the past year.
There is continued demand for residential properties in the Northern Beaches including single residential properties and quality boutique development sites with potential to build residential units. This is partly due to the Northern Beaches attractive natural amenity, beaches, and proximity to employment options.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Overall, there was a slight increase in commercial land values in the Northern Beaches local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This was not consistent in all commercial market segments.

Commercial sites with a Local Centre zoning in locations including Balgowlah, Balgowlah Heights, Fairlight, Manly and Seaforth showed a strong increase.

Some commercial sites with a Local Centre zoning at Dee Why Beach, overlooking Dee Why Beach, showed a strong increase.

Commercial sites with a Productivity Support zoning in locations including Balgowlah, Belrose, Brookvale, Dee Why, Manly and North Narrabeen showed a strong increase.

Demand remains for quality development sites, especially for development sites benefitting from proposed/planned improvements in infrastructure.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

There was a strong increase in the rural land values overall across the Northern Beaches local government area between July 2023 and July 2024. This increase was not consistent throughout the district as rural properties at Warriewood Valley evidenced slight land value increases.
Within the rural components, properties include standard homes on small holdings/isolated bush land sites to prestige homes with equine facilities and associated improvements. There has been higher demand for prestige rural properties. The rural market in this location have benefitted from markets placing greater emphasis on larger properties with attractive natural amenity and those that offer superior access to lifestyle conveniences. These rural properties are also well located to all services including hospitals and both public and private schools and major shopping precincts.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
