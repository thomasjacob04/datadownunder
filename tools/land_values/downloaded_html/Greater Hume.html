Murray region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Greater Hume area were prepared by Quotable Value Aust. P/L.

General overview

Land values in the Greater Hume local government area experienced a moderate decline between July 2023 and July 2024, following a period of consistent growth in previous years. This decline was not uniform across all market segments. The rural sector, which accounts for over 90% of the total land value in the area saw a strong decrease, driving the overall downward trend. Residential land values showed a moderate increase, while the industrial market segment, centred on the Jindera industrial estate, saw very strong increases.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall have shown a moderate increase. With flexible and remote working patterns being more normalised, regional cities and towns are becoming increasingly attractive to purchasers. The increase was not consistent across all market segments in the local government area. Residential village properties in Culcairn showed a very strong increase in land values. While the smaller village area of Burrumbuttock Village saw a slight decrease, other smaller and remote areas in the district such as Henty Commercial, Walbundrie, and Walla Walla saw land values remain stable. This comes after predominantly very strong increases in 2023. Large lot residential areas in Holbrook and Woomargama showed values remained stable after only a slight increase last year, but a very strong increase in 2022. Purchasers are attracted to these villages due to the country lifestyle, quiet location, and relatively affordable housing options that are available.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values in Jindera have seen a very strong increase after remaining stable in 2023. The Jindera industrial area benefits from the town's growing population, a stable employment base, and its proximity to the larger Albury-Wodonga centre. The industrial estate offers affordable, well-serviced sites, catering to both Jindera and the wider Albury-Wodonga region.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values have shown a strong decrease between July 2023 and July 2024, following several years of very strong increases. This movement was not consistent across the local government area. In recent years, demand for rural properties in the Greater Hume local government area has been driven by purchasers' long-term commitment to and confidence in the rural sector. For larger rural properties, many purchasers have been existing landholders looking to expand their farming operations. Additionally, accessibility and proximity to Albury-Wodonga, which offers a broad range of goods and services, continues to support demand in the Greater Hume area. Confidence in the rural property market has softened, and a degree of uncertainty is now evident among purchasers. As a result, properties are no longer achieving the premium prices seen during the last two years. Market conditions have shifted with potential purchasers displaying greater caution due to a range of economic conditions. This has contributed to a cooling of demand for rural properties.
Rural values in areas closer to Albury or with Murray River water frontage did not experience the strong decrease seen in other parts of Greater Hume, with values either remaining steady or showing strong to very strong increases. This increase in water frontage properties is primarily due to their proximity to Albury City and the desirable water views provided by Murray River frontage. Furthermore, several rural areas in the south-southeast and north corridors saw values remain steady or increase. Primary Production Small Lots in Gerogery, Jindera, Culcairn, Morven, Walla Walla, and Brocklesby also remained steady, except for Primary Production Small Lots with areas under 20 hectares in Jindera, which saw a slight increase. Typically, the areas experiencing the largest decreases in rural land values were those that had seen the strongest increases in recent years.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
