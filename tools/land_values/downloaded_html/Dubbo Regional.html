 Residential land values

Residential overview

There has been a slight increase in residential values between July 2023 and July 2024. Demand by homebuyers to relocate from cities to regional areas during rising of cost-of-living has provided a driver for increasing values, especially as working from home flexibilities have continued to be offered across many businesses. The strongest increases were seen to residential lots that have a minimum lot size of 600sqm within Wellington, as well larger residential lots located on Dubboâ€™s eastern fringe. Strong increases in land values were also experienced to larger residential allotments and englobo sites, residential blocks in south Wellington and land within Dubbo located in well regarded areas with a minimum lot size of 600sqm.

The exception to these decreases was land located in newer residential estates, as well as to residential allotments on the western side of Dubbo, principally developed for NSW Government housing estates. These types of properties both experienced slight decreases due to high interest rates, and significant rises to cost-of-living between July 2023 and July 2024.

Land values in villages across the Dubbo Regional local government area showed mixed performances, with the village of Mumbil seeing a strong increases over the past 12 months, while values within Eummungerie and sites in Geurie with a minimum lot size of 2,000sqm both experienced a moderate decrease. This decrease was due to the influence of the Dubbo residential market not experiencing as strong as an increase for 2024, which has therefore decreased the demand for more affordable land in surrounding villages such as Geurie and Eummungerie.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

The commercial market has been steady from July 2023 to July 2024 underpinned by a degree of uncertainty within this sector that reflects the general slowdown in consumer spending, leading and confidence. This has also led to cautious investment spending and decline in retail occupancy take up. Exceptions to this were the Dubbo commercial mixed use sector which has experienced a slight increase in value throughout this period due to an increase in demand for centrally located buildings with both residential and commercial use.
Properties land values located in the Dubbo CBD area have remained steady and although sought after by investors, the yields remained steady due to economic factors. Increased construction costs have become a deterrent where anticipated yields as a result of the rise in costs have softened, solidifying that impact to value. Whilst those factors have not led to any assessed decrease in the market there has been no evidence of a forward moving market throughout this period.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

The rural market experienced a slight increase from July 2023 to July 2024. Rural land values across the Dubbo Regional local government area have continued to experience upward pressure, resulting in continuous demand for rural property. Drivers of this increase include succession planning as well as purchasers coming from more marginal areas are looking to 'drought proof' their properties. This was evident by the very strong increase in values to rural irrigation properties within the local government area.
Exception s to the overall upward movement was to hobby farms, more specifically small allotments located approximately 20kms or more from Dubbo, which experienced a slight decrease. This decrease was due to these size allotments being located a distance from town and services and not considered to be in high demand.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
