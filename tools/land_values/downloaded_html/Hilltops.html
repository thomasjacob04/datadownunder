 Residential land values

Residential overview

The residential market in the Hilltops local government area showed a slight increase in land values between July 2023 and July 2024 which was mostly attributable to affordability. The southern end of Hilltops is within close proximity to the larger cities of Canberra, Wagga Wagga and southern Sydney and provides a more affordable option in comparison to these metropolitan areas.
The residential properties near the Young Golf Club, larger residential lots located on south of Dillion Street on the southern edge of Boorowa Township, and residential properties throughout Young with land areas ranging from 3000 sqm to 1 hectare all saw a significant increase in land value. This strong increase was due to the growing demand for more affordable housing options in Young. The strong economic conditions in rural areas, along with workers relocating to rural areas and in need of accommodation, drove up residential property prices with Young and the surrounding towns.
The villages of Reids Flat, Rye Park, Bribbaree and Rugby and Frogmore all showed a very strong decrease due to limited demand for allotments due to reduced buyers in the weekender market. These areas saw large increases in 2023 due to out of town buyers purchasing for weekender accommodation, however the high interest rates and cost of living has caused limited demand in the weekender market, therefore a decrease in the villages.
Variations to this upward movement include residential englobo land and hobby farms around Young and Harden all showed a moderate decrease in value. This decrease can be attributed to subdued demand due to slowing economic conditions and over supply , as well as high interest rates and fluctuating commodity prices.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

The rural market in the Hilltops local government area has remained steady, attributable to high interest rates and fluctuating commodity prices. Many farmers were choosing to pay down additional debt, instead of purchasing additional land. Following a strong economy for agriculture in recent years, we are now starting to see a stabilisation in the market as a result of lowering farming yields and increased economic uncertainty with cost-of-living pressures.
There was moderate to strong increase for grazing and mixed farming country to the north of Boorowa, after not increasing as much as surrounding rural area in 2023.
The value of non-arable land in Bribbaree locality was lowered, as the market preference for arable land that can be pasture improved or cropped. Consequently, we saw an increase of mixed farming throughout Bendick Murrell and Murringo localities.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
