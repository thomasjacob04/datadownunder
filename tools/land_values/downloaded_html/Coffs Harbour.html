 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, the residential land values were steady across the Coffs Harbour local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.

This trend was consistent throughout most of the residential areas due to continued demand for Coffs Harbour as a lifestyle destination.

The exceptions were in Sapphire Beach and properties located south of the Central Business District which showed a moderate decrease following consistent strong growth over previous years.

Park Beach, Emerald Beach, Korora, Sandy Beach (east of the highway), Safety Beach and Woolgoolga generally showed moderate to strong increases in land values. Improved network infrastructure following continued upgrades of the Pacific Highway and commencement of construction on the bypass coupled with proximity to the beaches and potential ocean views, have been influencing factors.

Bonville showed a moderate increase due to the continued development of the area surrounding the Bonville Golf Course and subsequent release of new lots in recent subdivisions.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Moonee Beach and Park Beach local centres showed slight increases as a result of more affordable land values and continued construction of the Coffs Harbour bypass which has contributed to the demand in these areas.

The local centres Corindi to Boambee experienced a strong increase due to a low value base in comparison to the surrounding commercial areas in addition to the continued construction of the Coffs Harbour bypass which has also contributed to the demand in these areas.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values in the Coffs Harbour local government area were generally steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

This was consistent across most of the rural market due to more subdued demand balanced by reasonable supply.

Rural holdings in the Corindi area including home sites, grazing properties and horticultural properties experienced a strong increase due to a low value base in 2023 in comparison to the surrounding rural areas.

Rural holdings in the Brooklana, Lowanna and Ulong areas including home sites, lifestyle properties and grazing properties experienced a moderate to strong increase due to relative affordability in comparison to most of the rural locations in the Coffs Harbour Local Government Area within proximity to the coast.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
