General overview

Overall, the Central Coast local government area experienced a slight increase in land values between July 2023 and July 2024.

This upward trend was experienced by the residential and rural markets, which showed a slight increase.

Commercial and industrial land values remained steady.

The market had been showing signs of recovery with a slight increase in growth observed after a period of declining values last year. This improvement can largely be attributed to the recent stabilisation in interest rates, coupled with anticipation of potential rate cuts.

The market recovery has been supported by increased demand from Sydney buyers, drawn by affordability and lifestyle options, boosting population growth and local housing demand.

In contrast, commercial centre zoned land experienced a moderate decrease due to subdued demand following large increases in recent years and moderate retail vacancy rates. Mixed Use zoned land experienced a slight decrease.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall, there was a slight increase in residential land values in the Central Coast local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.

The market had been showing signs of recovery with a slight increase in growth observed after a period of declining values last year. This improvement can largely be attributed to the recent stabilisation in interest rates, coupled with anticipation of potential rate cuts.

Long Jetty, Wyong, and Kariong have experienced a strong increase in land values, reflecting strategic planning and demand shifts. Wyong's growth is driven by density zoning and the introduction of Transport Oriented Development (TOD) reforms, encouraging development near railway lines. Kariongâ€™s proximity to the M1 Motorway and Sydney enhances its appeal for buyers seeking lifestyle and connectivity. Long Jetty saw strong growth in its higher density general residential and medium density residential zones due to its sought-after coastal location and larger land sizes allowing for low rise medium density development.

Areas which experienced a slight decrease in values included Gosford, West Gosford, The Entrance North, Ourimbah, Warnervale, Gorokan, Narara, Patonga, Blackwall, Booker Bay, Daleys Point, Chain Valley Bay, Kingfisher Shores, Charmhaven, Lake Haven, Mannering Park, and Toukley.

Overall, Killcare Heights has seen a moderate decrease in land values, primarily due to reduced demand for holiday homes, which traditionally support the local market. Additionally, the prevalence of sloping blocks with obstructed views due to tree cover has impacted values, as these properties also present accessibility and building challenges, further reducing their appeal in the current market.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Overall, there were steady industrial land values in the Central Coast local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.

There were variations to this trend however, with a moderate increase in the Warnervale precinct and a strong increase in the Bushells Ridge precinct. Limited release of new industrial land in the wider Central Coast region over recent years, has led to a long-term trend of consistently rising land values with an under-supply to the local market.

The completion of NorthConnex toll road has driven demand for Central Coast industrial land close to the M1 Motorway in areas such as Somersby and Warnervale, have effectively been the only available industrial land on offer. The demand for industrial land has also been supported by significant surrounding population growth within the northern precincts of the Central Coast including Warnervale, Wadalba, Hamlyn Terrace and Gwandalan.

More established precincts located further away from the M1 Motorway, which service surrounding local population needs, have not shown the same level of demand as industrial land closer to main transport links such as the M1 Motorway and the Pacific Highway.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Overall, there was a slight increase in rural land values in the Central Coast local government area between July 2023 and July 2024.

This trend was broadly consistent across rural locations, however, land values in Calga, Mount White, and Peats Ridge experienced a strong increase. This increase was attributed growth in rural appeal, as the area has become increasingly desirable due to its proximity to Sydney and the M1 Motorway.

Land values in Kulnura experienced a slight decrease. This is a less sought after area for rural property in the local government area and is considered to be a correction in the local market in terms of the relativities of value for this location.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
