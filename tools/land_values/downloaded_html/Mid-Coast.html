General overview

Overall, land values in the Mid-Coast local government area (LGA) have remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

This trend was consistent across residential and commercial properties whilst the industrial land values experienced a moderate increase, and the rural land values experienced a slight increase.

Moderate, overall increases in industrial land value were driven by industrial land supply shortages, continued demand for goods and services, and a sustained level of building activity. Whilst slight, overall rural increases were driven by buyers seeking lifestyle properties with growth potential within good proximity to the coast and the Pacific Highway.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values overall in the Mid-Coast LGA remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

General residential zoned properties located in Taree near the hospital, Tallwoods Village, and Seascape and Bombora subdivisions within the beachside village of Red Head and Tinonee experienced a moderate decrease. Whilst the beachfront properties located in Diamond Beach experienced a strong decrease. Similarly, low density zoned properties located in Forster, One Mile Beach area and Central and South Forster also experienced a moderate decrease in land values. Decreases in these areas can be associated with subdued demand, increased stock levels and elevated building costs.

Gloucester low density residential properties experienced a strong increase in land values due to an increase in demand for the rural residential lifestyle the area offers whilst being in proximity to town amenities and the coast.

Low density residential zoned properties located in Tuncurry and Winda Woppa experienced a moderate increase and the Hawks Nest properties experienced a strong increase in land values. General residential zoned properties located in Taree North, Harrington Waters and Hallidays Point Beachside Estate also experienced a moderate increase. These locations tend to attract buyers due to proximity to the coast and local shops within the tourism area of the Mid-Coast.

Medium density residential zoned properties within the coastal suburb of Hawks Nest experienced a strong increase in land values due to an increase in demand as a result of continued population growth in the area.

Large lot residential properties located in Tuncurry, North Gloucester, Barrington and Wingham experienced a moderate increase in land values. Properties located in Bulahdelah, Failford, Darawank and South Gloucester experienced a strong increase in land values. Increases were due to demand for lifestyle properties in close proximity to town centres and services.

The Taree mixed use properties located predominantly on the fringe of the Taree commercial centre, experienced a strong increase due to the objectives of the zone which allows for diversity in land uses that ranges from retail, office, light industrial, community to other non-residential land uses on the ground floor of buildings.

The Forster and Tuncurry mixed use properties located predominantly on the fringe of the Forster-Tuncurry commercial centres, experienced a moderate increase due to the objectives of the zone which allows for diversity in land uses that ranges from retail, office, light industrial, community to other non-residential land uses on the ground floor of buildings.

Strong increases were experienced in coastal and inland towns and villages including Barrington, Stroud, Stroud Road, Bulahdelah, Elands and Nerong. Moderate increases were experienced in the southern part of the LGA. The uplift in values were driven by buyers seeking lifestyle properties with growth potential and proximity to the coast, and access to the Pacific Highway.

A moderate decrease was evident for Smiths Lake Village lots. This decrease can be attributed to the reduced demand for properties in this location due to an increase in availability of lots closer to the beach. In addition, there has been an increase in supply of vacant lots with dwelling eligibility in this location however limited demand due to rising construction costs.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial land values overall in the Mid-Coast LGA remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024.

The Wingham local centre experienced a moderate increase in land values due to the underlying demand in the area and weakening supply. This area is relatively affordable compared to areas closer to the coast. Demand for commercial properties comes from a mixture of sources including 'tree changers' and lifestyle buyers from the larger cities, local buyers and property investors. Supply is limited due to the relatively small commercial centre.

The Taree, Forster and Cundletown productivity support precincts experienced moderate increase in land values due to the objectives of the zone which allows for diversity in land uses for a range of facilities and services, light industries, warehouses and offices.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

The rural property market in the Mid-Coast LGA has experienced a slight increase overall between 1 July 2023 and 1 July 2024.

Rural lands in areas such as Gloucester, Stroud, Barrington, Bundabah, Carrington and flood prone land in Wingham all experienced strong increases. Smaller rural holdings in Nabiac and Pindimar showed moderate increases. The increase in values is due to its relative affordability and steady demand from tree changers.

A moderate decrease in land values was evident for rural lifestyle and large grazing properties west of Wingham and a slight decrease was evident for rural residential properties in and around Oxley Island and Manning Point. These decreases are attributed to a decrease in demand for lifestyle and grazing properties due to a downturn in buyer sentiment combined with an increase in properties listed for sale in these localities.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
