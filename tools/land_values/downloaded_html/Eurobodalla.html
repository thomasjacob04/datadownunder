Land values in the Eurobodalla local government area

South Coast NSW region

The information in this interactive summary was prepared by Valuer General NSW. 2024 land values in the Eurobodalla area were prepared by Walsh & Monaghan Pty Ltd.

General overview

Values in the Eurobodalla local government area remained steady in general from July 2023 through to July 2024, characterised by relatively reduced sales volumes throughout the period.

Generally, residential and commercial areas have remained steady with strong increases shown in rural zones. Moderate increases in industrial zoned land have occurred throughout the period.

While there remains a steady demand by owner occupiers and investors looking for coastal locations within proximity of Sydney, Canberra and to a lesser extent Melbourne, rising inflation and increasing construction costs have resulted in a general steadying of various market sectors.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Values in most of the residential areas have remained steady from July 2023 through to July 2024 with relatively steady sales volumes throughout the period. This movement varies between locations.

Properties in Narooma, Broulee, Potato Point and Bodalla showed a moderate increase with increased demand and limited supply.

Strong increases were shown in development properties across the LGA, as interest is increasing for land in established residential areas.

In contrast, low density residential properties in Catalina, Sunshine Bay, Denhams Beach and Silverwater Estate Rosedale, moderately decreased in value compared to the previous year. Demand from purchasers has been impacted by broader economic factors.

Residential headland and beachfront properties have shown a strong decrease. These premium locations with high underlying land values have seen softened demand due to increases in holding costs and reduced affordability.

Southern villages medium density residential has experienced strong decreases, with this market impacted by the high cost of building, which drives down underlying site values.

In general, environmental living sites have varied depending on the location and the size of the property. Environmental living and large lot residential zoned lands in northern suburbs experienced strong decreases. These markets are driven by purchasers who are transitioning from conventional residential areas. Significant level of investment required and increased cost associated with borrowing in these areas has lessened demand

Southern environmental living sites experienced strong increases, due to strong demand and restricted supply for properties within these localities.

Overall, proximity to Canberra and major southern NSW regional centres, improved transport routes, and affordability for retirees and holiday home seekers are major drivers of market activity. However, demand has been tempered by rising interest rates, higher inflation and increased construction costs.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Values in most of the commercial areas have remained steady from July 2023 through to July 2024 with reduced sales activity throughout the period.

A limited supply and the tightly held nature of the commercial market has underpinned values in recent years, and commercial properties in smaller villages and centres continue to experience steady demand by owner occupiers and investors.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Values in most industrial areas have generally shown moderate increases from July 2023 through to July 2024, with reduced sales activity throughout the period.

Industrial land in Batemans and Moruya remained steady, however, Industrial properties from Bodalla to Narooma showed very strong increases. This increase is largely attributable to a lack of suitably zoned land within the Eurobodalla LGA for industrial development.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Values in most of the rural areas have shown strong increases compared to 2023 levels with reduced sales activity throughout the period. This movement is not consistent and varies across the LGA.

Exception to this are lifestyle properties within Eurobodalla, which showed overall slight increases compared to other rural holdings. Lifestyle properties to the northern and southern portions of the LGA remained steady while properties within the central portion of the LGA showed a slight increase.

Land value movements in the rural sector are considered partly attributable to a subdued residential market. Purchasers driving the demand in this sector are transitioning onto lifestyle properties after selling or leasing their properties in residential areas.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
