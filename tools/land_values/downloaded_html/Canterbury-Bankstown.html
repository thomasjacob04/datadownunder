General overview

Overall, there was a moderate increase in land values across the Canterbury Bankstown local government area from July 2023 to July 2024. This trend was not consistent across all sectors. The overall moderate increase across the local government area was driven by the residential sector with strong sales activity from increased demand and constrained stock levels.

 Residential land values

Residential overview

Overall residential land values in the Canterbury Bankstown local government area experienced a moderate increase from July 2023 to July 2024. This movement was most evident in properties comprising single residential home sites. Sale volumes in this sector have increased with adequate vacant land and improved sales evidence recorded this period to support value movements.

The increase in values can be attributed to an increase in market demand despite increased inflationary pressures experienced throughout the period. Higher sales activity, an increase in market demand and constrained stock levels have resulted in increased competition and rising sale prices. The Sydney Metro Southwest project will also contribute to improved transportation between Bankstown to Sydenham and is a driver for increases to the residential sector. Additionally, Canterbury Bankstown council has introduced the Connective City 2036 Plan which will serve for further growth within the local government area.

The exceptions to the moderate increase were larger residential zoned sites capable of medium density development located north of the Hume Highway. Demand overall in this period was lower and value levels have remained steady. This was also evident for high density zoned residential sites where properties that do not meet the minimum requirements to be developed on their own remained steady. Demand for these older style unit blocks has slowed.

The mixed use zoned properties located in Bankstown were another exception within the residential sector. These properties either showed a slight decrease or remained steady. Mixed use properties can also support commercial uses and these value movements align with the overall commercial value movement. Demand overall in this period was lower with mixed use zoned properties within Bankstown, due to generally being tightly held with low sales volumes.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial land values in Canterbury Bankstown local government area remained overall steady from July 2023 to July 2024.

The exceptions are local centre zoned sites in Campsie and business development zoned sites in Greenacre, Bass Hill and Villawood. Prime retail in the Campsie local centre showed a strong decrease due to market evidence showing a softening of values within the precinct. Whilst the secondary retail in Campsie local centre showed a moderate increase. The secondary local neighbourhood centre zoned sites in Campsie are included in the Campsie Town City Masterplan intensification areas where higher intensification can occur outside the primary core commercial.

The business development zone within the former Bankstown local government area showed a moderate increase. The sites are mostly used as hotel, fast food outlet, service station, office building and single residential dwellings. These are generally located on main roads with good exposure or access to main arterial roads.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
