 Residential land values

Residential overview

Residential land values across Tamworth Regional local government area experienced a moderate increase from July 2023 to July 2024.

The large lot residential sector experienced very strong increases for properties south of Tamworth, Manilla, Nundle and Hanging Rock.

Residential development properties, comprising mostly smaller sites located closer to the town centre, also experienced very strong increases. This a result of their relative affordability and forecast strong yields.

Generally larger residential development lands to the north of Tamworth remained steady due to their overall higher initial cost and larger expenditure to provide services.

The older area of Hillvue remained steady. This area is less attractive to first home buyers and investors compared to the neighbouring suburbs of South Tamworth and the West Tamworth and Coledale areas.

Manilla residential values have largely stabilized following moderate to strong increases over the past two years and ongoing interest rate rises and high inflation. With the median price now increased the township is becoming less affordable compared to other smaller villages within proximity to Tamworth City.

 Commercial land values

Commercial overview

Commercial land values across Tamworth Regional local government area experienced an overall slight increase from July 2023 to July 2024 reflecting stabilisation of supply and demand drivers.

Satellite commercial estates at Westdale, Taminda and Hillvue experienced a strong increase in land values, driven by demand for land in the newer industrial subdivision known as 'Tamworth Business Park’. This estate is popular due to the recently opened Intermodal Freight Facility located within the subdivision, its strategic location between Tamworth Airport and the established industrial precinct of Taminda, and accessibility from Oxley Highway.

The mixed-use precinct centred along Bridge Street, defined by primarily commercial development, experienced very strong increases. Properties without frontage to Bridge Street showed higher increases, reflecting an improvement in demand for more affordable properties.

 Industrial land values

Industrial overview

Industrial land values across Tamworth Regional local government area experienced a very strong increase between July 2023 and July 2024, driven by strong demand from both owner occupiers and investors. This was particularly evident within the established industrial area of Taminda, where limited stock is available.

The industrial area at Nemingha showed a strong increase while at Kootingal, the evidence indicated a moderate increase. These two small peripheral estates mainly service the surrounding residential and rural localities. The small industrial estate at Manilla remained steady with well-balanced supply and demand. This was consistent with the majority of other zones in the Manilla area.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Rural land values across Tamworth Regional local government area remained steady from July 2023 to July 2024.

Average seasonal conditions, continuing high interest rates and a generally more subdued commodity price outlook contributing to the present stable conditions.
Exceptions included mixed farms to the west and south of Tamworth which have experienced a moderate decrease. Purchasers active in this well-regarded location are predominately local land owners, with rising interest rates and inflated commodity input prices having some impact on availability of finance and overall demand. Conversely, mixed farms to the north of Tamworth have experienced a strong increase driven primarily by ongoing interest in the higher rainfall Horton Valley locality which has flowed through to the Barraba area.

Lifestyle properties around Barraba and Manilla township have experienced a moderate increase. This is driven by prior very strong increases to the Tamworth periphery lifestyle property sector, with these localities being more affordable.

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
