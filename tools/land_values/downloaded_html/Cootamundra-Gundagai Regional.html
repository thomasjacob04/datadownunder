 Residential land values

Residential overview

Cootamundra-Gundagai residential land values experienced a slight increase due to continued good seasonal conditions maintaining the visual and commercial appeal of the towns and villages, while also maintaining affordability.
This was not consistent across all residential segments with strong increases experienced in the medium density residential and larger lots in Gundagai attributed to its location in relation to the Hume Highway and metropolitan areas such as Canberra and Wagga Wagga with limited supply. Other residential segments in both Gundagai and Cootamundra townships have remained mostly steady due to current stock levels meeting demand after three years of increases.
Village properties showed a strong increase in Wallendbeen and Nangus and a very strong increase in Stockinbingal because of low supply and strong demand for this type of property due to relative affordability compared to larger towns.

 Rural land values

Rural overview

Cootamundra-Gundagai rural land values remained steady between July 2023 and July 2024. The steady nature of the market in this local government area can be attributed to falling demand however offset by a limited supply of listings for this type of property. After experiencing strong increases in recent years, mixed farming in Cootamundra and Gundagai has remained steady due to falling livestock prices coupled with inflationary pressure on farm input costs and rising interest rates impacting on buyer confidence and borrowing capacity. These factors have all contributed to a more subdued outlook for rural land values.
Small rural holdings and rural homesites around Gundagai and hobby blocks around Wallendbeen showed a strong increase in values while small lots around Cootamundra showed a moderate increase. This type of property remains desirable due to affordability when compared to small rural holdings near Wagga and Canberra

 Added value of improvement tables

The added value of improvement tables show dollar values per square metre for the added value of improvements for the main residential property types in a local government area. It is the value the improvements add to the value of the land as demonstrated by the analysis of property sales.


Read more

Land value is the value of your land only. It does not include the value of your home or other structures. The term added value of improvements is the dollar value that your home or other structures add to land value.

The added value of improvements is their worth as demonstrated by the property market in a local government area and is generally not equal to the replacement or insurance value.

The added values of improvements are determined by the analysis of a broad range of market sales including vacant land and properties with improvements in a range of conditions, ages, sizes and styles. The sale prices of the range of properties is then compared. Where the land is comparable the difference in sale price is the added value of the improvements.

This analysis is then included in a table identifying different building types on a dollar rate per square metre basis. Those rates are then used to deduct the added value of the improvements from other sales in the area to determine their land values.

More information on this "paired sales analysis" approach is contained in the Valuer General's policy on the 'Valuation of Single Residential Land' available on our website at valuergeneral.nsw.gov.au.
